Challenges

This project also highlights several important challenges in building a real-world dataspace. One challenge is interoperability: data from different providers must be described, discovered, and exchanged in a consistent way. Another challenge is governance: access rules must remain meaningful after the data leaves the provider’s domain. There is also an institutional challenge, since different organizations may have different priorities, risk concerns, and technical capabilities. Finally, real-time service scenarios introduce additional constraints, such as low-latency updates, limited caching, and stronger requirements for data freshness and attribution. These challenges make the project more than a simple data integration exercise; they show why dataspace design requires both technical architecture and governance logic to work together.
