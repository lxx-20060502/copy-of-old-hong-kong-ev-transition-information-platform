Value

The value of this project lies in both its technical and conceptual contributions. Technically, it demonstrates how a federated dataspace can support interoperable data sharing without forcing providers to surrender control of their assets to a centralized platform. Conceptually, it shows that public-sector data sharing can be designed as a governed process shaped by contractual terms, institutional roles, and policy enforcement. This is especially important in EV-related ecosystems, where multiple actors need timely access to data, but unrestricted circulation may create legal, operational, or trust-related concerns. By combining federated discovery with governed access, the project presents a practical model for balancing usability and control.
