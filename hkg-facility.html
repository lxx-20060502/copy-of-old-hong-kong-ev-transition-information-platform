New products are coming soon.

Hong Kong International Airport has already required all airside saloon vehicles to be electric since July 2017, has electrified 95% of its ground services equipment, and is moving toward converting all electrifiable airside vehicles by 2030. Its live deployment of autonomous electric tractors also signals that airport mobility is evolving from conventional transport support into a smarter, data-driven operational system.

Against this background, our product aims to provide a more structured view of airport-area electric mobility services and supporting facilities. Rather than focusing only on vehicles or only on charging, it is designed to integrate information about nearby charging availability, operational support facilities, access points, service zones, and mobility-related infrastructure surrounding the airport. The goal is to make this information easier to discover, interpret, and use for airport operators, service partners, fleet managers, and future mobility platforms.

In its envisioned form, the product would support airport operations in three ways. First, it could improve operational visibility by bringing fragmented facility and service information into one coordinated layer. Second, it could support decision-making for electric fleet deployment, routing, and service planning around airport demand patterns. Third, it could create a foundation for smarter airport mobility services, especially as electric and autonomous ground transport becomes more common in and around airport environments.

Our broader objective is to build a service-oriented information layer for airport e-mobility. In the near term, this means organizing infrastructure and service data into a usable product interface. In the longer term, it could support more advanced applications such as airport-area mobility coordination, facility planning, and ecosystem-level analysis for low-carbon transport operations. Rather than presenting a finished solution, this product is being developed as an exploratory step toward a more connected, data-enabled airport mobility environment.
